Seller Lab — Onboarding flow

Overview

Every seller on noon starts here — creating their store, submitting documents, passing KYC review, and eventually going live. For years this funnel had significant drop-off at every stage: confusing document flows, opaque review status, no in-context guidance, and a KYC bottleneck that drove the highest complaint volume on the platform.

I led the UX redesign of the onboarding flow: store creation, LE document submission, approval status visibility, and in-flow seller guidance. The work also involved service design — surfacing and fixing the process gaps that no amount of UI polish could hide.

The Problem

Sellers were dropping out at every stage of the funnel

The onboarding flow had been built incrementally and showed it. Each stage had its own friction — and the cumulative effect was that the majority of sellers who created a store never made it to live.

  • 📄
    Confusing document submission The LE (Legal Entity) approval flow had unclear field labels, missing guidance on required document formats, and no feedback when something was wrong — leading to repeated resubmissions and long wait times.
  • 🕐
    Opaque KYC review status Sellers had no visibility into where their application was in review. The KYC bottleneck was the loudest complaint in Q4 2025 — multi-week waits with no status update, and agents giving conflicting information.
  • 🔍
    Guidance existed — but wasn't discoverable Sellers rated negatively because they "didn't know where to apply" even though all relevant links and instructions already existed on the platform. The gap wasn't missing content — it was surfacing it at the right moment in the flow.
  • 📞
    Poor support reachability Sellers needed human contact but the platform funnelled them into email-only support. Free-text feedback showed sellers wanting callbacks and direct contact — a recurring complaint across all markets.
Process & Key Decisions

Four design moves that shifted the funnel

Decision 01
Redesign the LE submission flow around clarity, not completeness
The document submission form tried to explain everything upfront — a wall of instructions that sellers skipped. The redesign restructured it around progressive disclosure: show what's needed, when it's needed, with inline validation rather than end-of-form error dumps. Field labels were rewritten to match how sellers actually describe their documents, not how the system categorised them.
↑ Store → LE Approved rate: 31.5% → 53.1% (+21.6pp) in UAE · Q1 YoY
Decision 02
Make review status visible and specific — not just "pending"
Sellers in KYC review had no idea where they were or what to expect. The redesign introduced a visible review status tracker with named stages, estimated timelines, and a clear explanation of what happens next. Alongside the UI, an automated LE aging escalation process was introduced — replacing a manual, error-prone workflow where applications could sit unreviewed for weeks.
↑ CSAT: 3.03 → 3.31 (+9%) · Detractors: 45% → 33% (−12pp) · KYC complaints largely disappeared in Q1 2026
Decision 03
Surface guidance in-flow — don't send sellers to a help centre
The insight from seller feedback was sharp: the problem wasn't that guidance didn't exist, it was that sellers never found it when they needed it. The solution was in-context — inline checklists, progress nudges, and contextual help at the specific step where sellers were getting stuck. Rather than a "Help" link in the nav, guidance appeared as part of the flow.
Directly addresses the top discoverability complaint from seller CSAT free-text
Decision 04
Build a personalised seller dashboard to anchor the post-signup experience
After store creation, sellers had no clear "what's next." The dashboard gave them a personalised home — outstanding actions, document status, onboarding checklist, and a seller guide tailored to their store type. This reduced the disorientation that caused many sellers to abandon the process after initial sign-up.

What's next

The onboarding funnel improved — post-live is the new frontier

The data tells a clear story: more sellers are completing onboarding, but fewer are making their first sale. In UAE, first-sale conversion declined from 16.1% to 10.9% (−5.3pp), and LTV3 per store fell from AED 4,072 to AED 2,515 (−38%). The design problem has shifted from "getting sellers live" to "getting live sellers selling."

Open design problems from the data
  • Post-live seller visibility and discovery — organic visibility for long-tail sellers lags behind competitors
  • Chinese-language seller UX — opaque rejection reasons, missing field labels (PRJ/ORG numbers), no CN-language support layer; drove CSAT to 2.79 in partial Q2 data
  • First-sale conversion — onboarding is smoother but post-live monetisation support lags the funnel improvements
  • Support reachability — sellers want callbacks and direct contact, not email-only; consistently the #1 pain point in free-text feedback
